The Ace Attorney Game Series

Introduction

Ace Attorney is a visual novel adventure game series created by Capcom. It follows the protagonist, defense attorney Phoenix Wright, and his court adventures, where he battles to save his clients. It includes 6 mainline games: the Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney Trilogy and the Apollo Justice: Ace Attorney Trilogy. Plus 4 spin-off games: Ace Attorney Investigations Collection, where our protagonist is prosecutor Miles Edgeworth, and The Great Ace Attorney Chronicles, where our protagonist is Phoenix Wright’s ancestor, Ryunosuke Naruhodo.

I have either played or watched gameplays of all the games except for The Great Ace Attorney Chronicles, but that doesn't mean I can't list down my favorites. I will talk about some of my favorite games in the series, the cases in it which I liked the most, and my favorite characters from them. (The listed games are not in any specific order, I just like them)

There will probably be spoilers.

Phoenix Wright: Trials and Tribulations

"Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney – Trials and Tribulations is a visual novel adventure video game developed and published by Capcom. It was originally released for the Game Boy Advance in 2004 in Japan, and has since been released for several platforms, including a Nintendo DS version that was released in 2007 in Japan and North America, and in 2008 in Europe.

The story follows defense attorneys Phoenix Wright and Mia Fey, who defend their clients in five episodes. Among other characters are Phoenix's assistant and Mia's sister Maya, her cousin Pearl, and prosecutor Godot."

I liked this game a lot, gameplay-wise? It's pretty much the same as the classic Phoenix Wright games, story-wise? It's honestly the best in the trilogy. The episodes of the game were not in chronological order, so you had to put them together yourself. So here is my favorite case:

Episode 5: Bridge to the Turnabout

The last case and the best one. Phoenix Wright, Maya Fey, and Pearl Fey visit the snowy Hazakura Temple so Maya can complete her spirit medium training. While there, Phoenix meets a nun named Iris who looks exactly like his ex-girlfriend, now convicted murderer, Dahlia Hawthrone. He also meets a children's book author, Elise Deauxnim, who is actually Mia Fey and Maya’s long-lost mother, Misty Fey, in disguise.

Later that night, Elise Deauxnim is found dead, stabbed in the temple courtyard. At the same time, Dusky Bridge—which is the only way to get to the Inner Temple where Maya was training— is struck by lightning and is set on fire. Phoenix tries to cross the burning bridge to save Maya but falls into the freezing river below and is hospitalized.

With Phoenix stuck in the hospital, he asks his friend and rival prosecutor to defend Iris in court, because she was now being suspected of the murder. Edgeworth agrees, taking Phoenix’s attorney badge and magatama. (The magatama is a spiritual object that allows the user to see when people are keeping secrets. Psyche-Locks appear when a secret is being kept, and the more Psyche-Locks there are, the deeper the secret.)

While investigating, Edgeworth finds out the head nun, Sister Bikini, claims she saw Iris stabbing Elise. He is also given a sketch from Larry Butz depicting what he saw that night: A person flying over the bridge. While in court, it is revealed that Iris and Dahlia are twin sisters and daughters of Morgan Fey. Morgan had planned for Pearl to channel Dahlia’s spirit to kill Maya so Pearl could become the Fey Clan Master. The first trial ends.

Phoenix soon recovers and returns to his position as defense. During the second trial, we find out that Dahlia Hawthorne’s spirit has actually been channeled and is possessing a body to torment Maya. At first, it is suspected that Pearl was doing the channeling, but it is later debunked when they discover Maya had channeled her first and locked herself in the Inner Temple to keep Dahlia from hurting anybody.

This enrages Dahlia, who demands to know who had known to foil her and her mother’s plans. Pearl channels someone, and Mia Fey reveals herself as the mastermind. This makes Dahlia even madder because Mia was the one who had her thrown into prison and executed in the first place. Dahlia is later exorcised out of Maya’s body.

In the end, it is revealed that the prosecutor, Godot, is actually Diego Armando, Mia Fey’s former lover. Armando had been stuck in a 5-year coma after being poisoned by Dahlia. When he woke up, he found out that Mia was dead and Dahlia was in death row, making it feel like he had nothing to do in the world anymore. By being a prosecutor, he found out about Morgan Fey’s plot and went to the temple to "protect" Maya with the help of Misty and Iris. However, his desire for revenge against Dahlia consumed him.

Godot accepts his guilt, acknowledging that he was trying to play the hero to make up for failing to protect Mia years ago. He declares that Phoenix has proven himself a worthy successor to Mia Fey.
