The Ace Attorney Game Series

Introduction

Ace Attorney is a visual novel adventure game series created by Capcom. It follows the protagonist, defense attorney Phoenix Wright, and his court adventures, where he battles to save his clients. It includes 6 mainline games: the Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney Trilogy and the Apollo Justice: Ace Attorney Trilogy. Plus 4 spin-off games: Ace Attorney Investigations Collection, where our protagonist is prosecutor Miles Edgeworth, and The Great Ace Attorney Chronicles, where our protagonist is Phoenix Wright’s ancestor, Ryunosuke Naruhodo.

I have either played or watched gameplays of all the games except for The Great Ace Attorney Chronicles, but that doesn't mean I can't list down my favorites. I will talk about some of my favorite games in the series, the cases I liked most, and my favorite characters. (The listed games are not in any specific order; I just like them)

There will definitely be spoilers. Excuse me if I wrote too much. I like these games.

Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney

"Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney is a visual novel adventure game developed and published by Capcom. It was released in 2001 for the Game Boy Advance in Japan and has been ported to multiple platforms. The 2005 Nintendo DS version, titled Gyakuten Saiban Yomigaeru Gyakuten in Japan, introduced an English language option, and was the first time the game was released in North America and Europe

The story follows Phoenix Wright, a rookie defense attorney who attempts to have his clients declared "not guilty". Among other characters are Phoenix's boss, Mia Fey; his assistant and Mia's sister, Maya; and prosecutor Miles Edgeworth. "


First ever Ace Attorney Game and obviously a classic. We get introduced to Phoenix, Maya, Mia, and Edgeworth in the game.

Rise from the Ashes

This case was added in the DS remake; it's honestly a great addition and a beloved case.

The story begins two months after assistant Maya Fey has left Phoenix and gone to continue spirit channeling training. Phoenix is approached by Ema Skye, who ends up reminding him a lot of Maya. She is the sister of Lana Skye, the Chief Prosecutor. Lana is accused of stabbing Detective Bruce Goodman to death in the Underground Parking Lot of the Prosecutor’s Office.

Phoenix accepts Ema’s request to defend her sister and goes to meet his defendant in the detention center. There, Lana refuses to cooperate, claiming that she was guilty and that there was nothing they could do to defend her. She also acts cold towards her sister. Ema mentions that Lana has been like that ever since she became a prosecutor.

The investigation quickly spirals into a confusing mess when it is discovered that another "murder" involving the same victim, Bruce Goodman, allegedly took place at the exact same time at the Police Department. Phoenix, in court, discovers a connection with the current case and with the “SL-9 Incident” two years ago.

With a bit of help from Edgeworth and a lot from Ema and her skills in "scientific investigation" (dusting for fingerprints and using luminol to discover blood), they discover that the case was supposedly "solved" by Lana Skye and the legendary Chief of Police, Damon Gant. Yet, it seems as if it was not done by official means.

As the trial progresses, Phoenix realizes that Gant is the mastermind behind everything. Gant has been using the secret of what really happened during the SL-9 Incident to blackmail Lana into doing his bidding at the Prosecutor's Office for years. Gant had evidence that proved Ema was the killer of prosecutor Neil Marshall, and used this to force Lana to do his bidding. She became a prosecutor to continue helping him cover up his crimes.

Phoenix eventually proves that Gant was the one who actually killed Bruce Goodman to cover up the original evidence forgery and then framed Lana, knowing she would keep quiet to protect her sister.

Gant is eventually brought down when he is tricked into admitting he touched a piece of evidence he shouldn't have known about. It is revealed that it was he who killed Neil Marshall as well, because he wanted the serial killer Joe Darke convicted so badly. He forged evidence of Ema being a murderer to get Lana to help him, then forged even more evidence to get Joe Darke a guilty sentence.

Gant is arrested, and while Lana is cleared of the murder charge, she is arrested for her role in the evidence forgery of the past. Lana apologises for the hurt she has caused, and Ema says she forgives her. The sisters share a heartfelt hug, and Phoenix reminisces about another pair of sisters. Edgeworth realizes he doesn’t want to become someone like Gant and decides to “choose death” and find out who he should truly be.

I loved how Ema and Lana paralleled Mia and Maya, and also the fact that Mia used to be close friends with Lana made it hit more. Ema at first seems like just a Maya copy created to advance the story. She was, but in future games, you see her again, and I promise she is a different, more developed character.

It was the first case that ever stuck with me; it showed the deeper corruption within the law world right after we were done dealing with Manfred von Karma in the previous episode. Damon Gant was a great detective and chief of police, sullied by his need to put every single criminal behind bars, going as far as to kill and frame innocents to do so.

Blackmailing Lana with her love for her sister was a cruel act, and Gant will definitely be behind bars for the rest of his life until the death penalty comes. I'm glad the sisters managed to reunite and make up in the end; it would've been really sad if it ended up like the Fey sisters.

I would say Lana and Gant were my favorite characters in this case. Lana's love for her sister made her commit stupid but very understandable decisions. Gant was blackmailing her and threatening her little sister; I can understand what she was going through those two years. She had no one to talk to about her predicament, and any slip-up would end with Gant fulfilling his threat.

Gant, being a righteous hero who ended up corrupting himself, is a tale as old as time. Although not perfectly displayed, I enjoyed his character arc, flaws and all.

Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney - Trials and Tribulations

"Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney – Trials and Tribulations is a visual novel adventure video game developed and published by Capcom. It was originally released for the Game Boy Advance in 2004 in Japan, and has since been released for several platforms, including a Nintendo DS version that was released in 2007 in Japan and North America, and in 2008 in Europe.

The story follows defense attorneys Phoenix Wright and Mia Fey, who defend their clients in five episodes. Among other characters are Phoenix's assistant and Mia's sister Maya, her cousin Pearl, and prosecutor Godot."


I liked this game a lot, gameplay-wise? It's pretty much the same as the other classic Phoenix Wright games, story-wise? It's honestly the best in the trilogy. Playing as Mia was so nice. It makes me feel so sad that she died so early; she really had a whole life ahead of her. I'm very convinced that if she were still alive, she would've been a legendary attorney.

Bridge to the Turnabout

The last case and the best one. Phoenix Wright, Maya Fey, and Pearl Fey visit the snowy Hazakura Temple so Maya can complete her spirit medium training. While there, Phoenix meets a nun named Iris who looks exactly like his ex-girlfriend, now convicted murderer, Dahlia Hawthrone. He also meets a children's book author, Elise Deauxnim.

Later that night, Elise Deauxnim is found dead, stabbed in the temple courtyard. At the same time, Dusky Bridge—which is the only way to get to the Inner Temple where Maya was training—is struck by lightning and is set on fire. Phoenix tries to cross the burning bridge to save Maya but falls into the freezing river below and is hospitalized.

With Phoenix stuck in the hospital, he asks his friend and rival prosecutor to defend Iris in court, because she was now being suspected of the murder. Edgeworth agrees, taking Phoenix’s attorney badge and magatama. (The magatama is a spiritual object that allows the user to see when people are keeping secrets. Psyche-Locks appear when a secret is being kept, and the more Psyche-Locks there are, the deeper the secret.)

While investigating, Edgeworth finds out the head nun, Sister Bikini, claims she saw Iris stabbing Elise. He is also given a sketch from Larry Butz depicting what he saw that night: A person flying over the bridge. While in court, it is revealed that Iris and Dahlia are twin sisters and daughters of Morgan Fey. Morgan had planned for Pearl to channel Dahlia’s spirit to kill Maya so Pearl could become the Fey Clan Master. The first trial ends.

Phoenix soon recovers and returns to his position as defense. During the second trial, we find out that Elise is actually Mia and Maya’s long-lost mother, Misty Fey. Later, we also discover that Dahlia Hawthorne’s spirit has actually been channeled and is possessing a body to torment Maya. At first, it is suspected that Pearl was doing the channeling, but it is later debunked when they discover Maya had channeled her first and locked herself in the Inner Temple to keep Dahlia from hurting anybody.

This enrages Dahlia, who demands to know who had known to foil her and her mother’s plans. Pearl channels someone, and Mia Fey reveals herself as the mastermind. This makes Dahlia even madder because Mia was the one who had her thrown into prison and executed in the first place. Dahlia is later exorcised out of Maya’s body.

In the end, it is revealed that the prosecutor, Godot, is actually Diego Armando, Mia Fey’s former lover. Armando had been stuck in a 5-year coma after being poisoned by Dahlia. When he woke up, he found out that Mia was dead and Dahlia was in death row, making it feel like he had nothing to do in the world anymore. By being a prosecutor, he found out about Morgan Fey’s plot and went to the temple to "protect" Maya with the help of Misty and Iris. However, his desire for revenge against Dahlia consumed him.

Misty had channeled Dahlia first so that Pearl could not. Dahlia, now back in the living world, disguised herself as her twin sister and tried to kill Maya. Godot was on the scene protecting her, but in the process, he stabbed Dahlia, killing Misty. He then tries to cover up the scene by removing the blood. Unfortunately for him, he cannot see the color red when it is on a white background, making him mess up. This leads to the eventual revelation of his guilt.

Godot accepts his guilt, acknowledging that he was trying to play the hero to make up for failing to protect Mia years ago. He declares that Phoenix has proven himself a worthy successor to Mia Fey.

After the trial, Phoenix is deeply saddened that he did not get to save Godot, but Mia tells him that showing him he was wrong was the only way to save him. She tells Phoenix that she has nothing left to teach him and bids him farewell, saying that they will meet again someday.

I was so happy that we got to play as Mia. I wanted to know more about her, and playing as her in court just made me love her more.

Definitely one of my favorite reveals in Phoenix's games, I could not have suspected that it was Godot behind it all. It was obvious that he was Armando, but the murder part? I could not figure that out. I loved how Dahlia was introduced, how the story set her up from the first episode, and the big reveal where she came back in the last. She is still one of my favorite main antagonists out of all the games.

If it was not obvious, my favorite characters are indeed Dahlia and Godot. A woman raised without the love of parents, who eventually grew to care only for herself, committing multiple crimes, was defeated by Mia Fey, and this foolish guy, who had nothing going for him, so he had to opt for revenge in the worst way possible, and was later defeated by Phoenix Wright. A great duo if you ask me.

Not being able to truly save Armando is just sad, because he's implied to be dead after the game. He would never actually move on from Mia, and his misogynistic views are what led him down his dangerous path in the first place. I'm glad Phoenix showed him that he was being an idiot, but his last time ever seeing Mia being her against him in court made me feel bad.

Ace Attorney Investigations 2: Prosecutor's Gambit

"Ace Attorney Investigations 2: Prosecutor's Gambit[a] is an adventure video game developed by Capcom. It was released in Japan for the Nintendo DS in 2011 and for Android and iOS in 2017.

The game follows prosecutor Miles Edgeworth, detective Dick Gumshoe, and the teenage thief Kay Faraday, who investigate five cases; they face off against judge Verity Gavèlle, a rival character who is part of a "prosecutor purge" that removes weaker prosecutors from duty.



Prosecutor Miles Edgeworth is our main character, a close friend of Phoenix and the reason why he even started practicing law. I really like investigations, especially because of the different mechanics and the fact that you can move around and control a mini Edgeworth rather than pressing a button that says, "Go to Detention Center" or something along those lines. It's actually more enjoyable investigating in this game, although I have a bone to pick with "Mind Chess." Seeing Edgeworth's inner thoughts just makes him a more lovable guy, especially with the new cast of characters you see him get attached to. The game also brings back my biggest favorite character of all of Ace Attorney, Franziska von Karma.

Turnabout Legacy

Here you play as flipping defense attorney, Gregory Edgeworth, Edgeworth's dead dad. He's the reason why Edgeworth started practicing law, although his death led his son to the path of prosecutor under Manfred von Karma rather than defense. I loved playing as Gregory, he's actually a pretty funny guy.

The story is split between the past and the present, beginning eighteen years before the present. Gregory is assigned to defend a top confectioner named Samson Tangaroa, who has been accused of murdering a competitor, Artie Frost, during his own dessert-making competition.

Gregory investigates the crime scene with his assistant, Eddie Fender, and later, Detective Tyrell Badd as well. Our first suspect is Delicia Scone, one of the competitors, who is revealed along the investigation as a pharmacist rather than a confectioner. She had no knowledge of confectionery and was helped by Judy Bound to get to the finals. Judy had done this to keep her father safe.

The investigation is eventually put to a stop when prosecutor Manfred von Karma comes in and makes sure Gregory cannot get any more information out of the witnesses. Gregory manages to find out that Carmelo Gusto—another competitor—and Frost had been secretly working together to win the competition. Frost, just like Scone, was not a confectioner but rather a sculptor; he and Gusto worked together to win.

Gregory tries to pursue more information about the partnership but is stopped by von Karma, who deems it insignificant to the case. Things take a turn for the worse when they go into Frost’s competition room and find his confections gone. Judy admits to accidentally melting them. Later, they found out the body of Artie Frost had disappeared, creating a rather fickle case.

The trial continues for a year due to the loss of evidence, and von Karma uses a fake autopsy report to keep it going. In the end, Samson is pronounced guilty, and Gregory leaves with his son, which leads to the tragic “DL-6 Incident.”

In the present day, Edgeworth returns to the scene of his father’s final case to solve the mystery that Gregory could not finish before his death. He is brought there by Fender, who has come to pay a visit to his old friend Judy, as well as to reinvestigate the case. The melted statues of Artie Frost were apparently replicated and are being displayed in the gallery.

Then something bad happens, and Carmelo Gusto is found passed out after a gas attack in the Autumn wing. Edgeworth begins his investigation of the poison attack and finds Delicia Scone as well. It seemed that the scene of the crime 18 years ago was coming back together. They find out that Judy had been the one to plant the poison, and it was done as a trap to catch the murderer who framed her father.

The body of Artie Frost had been hidden with his statues, and those statues had been saved by Judy 18 years ago, although Judy did not know the body was there. When she found out, she planned to find the true killer by opening the gallery. The true killer has been revealed to be Gusto.

Gusto at first denies it, but Edgeworth soon gets the truth out of him. Gusto had a sickness that led to him not being able to taste, which was why he needed the competition’s prize, a recipe book for medicine. Frost had sabotaged him by getting his own son to make sure Gusto’s son never got to see his father in the finals. Gusto’s son was the one tasting his father’s food to make sure it tasted good.

Gusto found out about the betrayal, and Frost mocked his sickness. This led to them fighting and Frost’s death. Gusto is arrested, and so is Judy. Edgeworth and Fender visit Samson in prison and tell him everything. Samson is saddened by Judy resorting to crime just to save him, but is glad that his daughter truly loved him, and he loved her too.

Edgeworth has solved the case that led to his father’s unfortunate death, and he is also grappling with the fact that his prosecutor’s badge might be taken away from him. Fender tells him that he has to choose between being a prosecutor and following his father’s footsteps as a defense attorney.

I loved this case, literally amazing. It's my current favorite case in Ace Attorney, which will probably change when I play TGAA Chronicles, but goodness do I love it. The changes of POV between Edgeworth and Gregory and the cliffhanger you are left on each time were exhilarating. I even got so impatient when it came to Edgeworth's parts because I wanted to go back to playing as his dad.

The investigations were great, and so were the characters. Samson Tangaroa and his daughter Judy Bound were bittersweet; they love each other so much. The number of adoptive daughters in the entire game series seems to grow the more games I play.

Similar to Mia Fey, Gregory died too early, so we never really knew much about him other than surface details, but playing as him just gives him so much more character than before. I love his dynamic with Eddie Fender (I still think his fan translated name, Raymond Shields, sounds a million times better), and Detective Badd(we see more of him in the previous game), they make a great duo, actually.

The fact that nobody during Gregory's case knew what the heck was going on—including the great Manfred von Karma—just makes it a lot more lovable. Disappearing statues, disappearing bodies, all done by Judy to make sure her father one day gets set free, man, she's amazing.

Delicia Scone's ridiculous nicknames for everybody made me laugh harder, especially how funny Badd and Gregory react when being called "Baddy-Bumpkin" and "Greggy Pegleg" respectively. They have a great dynamic going for them here.

Case-wise, my favorite characters would genuinely be Gregory and Badd. Game-wise, include Franziska von Karma to that list; she only appears for two episodes, and we barely see her, but who cares?

Gregory and Badd's dynamic was lovely. I enjoyed their little banter and how Badd seems to just keep helping Gregory while acting like he doesn't care. He even had his pay deducted for helping too much.

If Gregory were still alive, I know he and Badd would make such an amazing investigating duo, even if Gregory isn't a Prosecutor. I don't think Badd cares about that, though. Gregory loved his son, and damn does it feel more sad knowing he's dead. I also feel sad about never seeing Badd ever again, man, did he really have to get arrested in the previous game?

Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney - Spirit of Justice

"Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney – Spirit of Justice is a visual novel adventure video game developed and published by Capcom. The game was released for Nintendo 3DS in Japan in June 2016, and in North America and Europe in September that year. Versions for iOS and Android followed a year later in September 2017, and it was released for Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 4, Windows, and Xbox One in January 2024 as part of the Apollo Justice: Ace Attorney Trilogy compilation.

The game primarily follows Phoenix Wright as he travels to the fictitious Kingdom of Khura'in to see Maya Fey, only to come into conflict with the country's court system, where defense attorneys have been deemed unnecessary, and verdicts are decided based on a mystical vision called the "Divination Séance". Meanwhile, Phoenix's subordinates, Apollo Justice and Athena Cykes, take on cases at the Wright Anything Agency in his absence."

This is the latest game in the main storyline and the last of the Apollo Justice trilogy. The game had so many new mechanics added that it just had me stumped. I was confused in most of the cases, but it was really fun to play; the name puns get more ridiculous, and so do the culprit breakdowns. I think again, my favorite case would be the last, which is usually the best one. This case was way too long, but hey, I enjoyed myself.

Turnabout Revolution

It was between this case and The Magical Turnabout. Unfortunately, the culprit in The Magical Turnabout is a weirdo, so I chose this instead.

We are greeted with the appearance of Dhurke Sahdmadhi, the leader of the Khura’inist revolution. He comes in as a client and is there to unite with his foster son, Apollo Justice. Apollo is unwelcoming at first, but begrudgingly listens to what Dhurke has to say. Dhurke reveals his mission in the United States is to retrieve the Founder’s Orb, a sacred Khura’inist relic currently in the possession of an archaeologist named Dr. Archie Buff.

However, when they arrive at his home to receive the orb, they find Dr. Buff dead, and a corrupt, arrogant politician named Paul Atishon claims the orb as a family heirloom. A bit of investigation leads to a kind of father-son bonding time. Apollo and Dhurke eventually find the orb in a cave, but are later intercepted by Atishon, who demands they settle the feud in court. This leads to a civil trial where Apollo must face his own mentor, Phoenix Wright, who, for some reason, has chosen to defend Atishon.

Phoenix is uncharacteristically ruthless during the trial because he is being blackmailed. The Khura’inist Minister of Justice has kidnapped Maya Fey, and her life depends on Phoenix securing the orb for Atishon. Despite the crazy pressure, Apollo stays true to his beliefs and proves that Atishon murdered Dr. Buff.

The group: Apollo, Phoenix, Athena Cykes(A fellow lawyer and another student of Phoenix), Edgeworth, and Dhurke, travel to the Kingdom of Khura’in, where the hostage exchange at the royal palace goes horribly wrong. The Minister of Justice, Inga Karkhuul Khura’in, is discovered dead in the Tomb of the Holy Mother, and Dhurke is found standing over the body with a knife, leading to his immediate arrest.

Apollo prepares to defend his foster father in a court presided over by the terrifying Queen Ga’ran. He is forced to deal with the "Divination Séances" (Seances where the Princess Rayfa Padma Khura’in shows the victim's last memories; it is usually done to falsely incriminate the accused) and a legal system ready to send him to the nearest chopping block if he dares to mess up. Dhurke manages to escape custody during the trial; the reason was to rescue his love from the royal family’s prison. It is soon discovered that the supposed dead queen, Queen Amara, who is the person Dhurke had wanted to rescue, was alive and was, in fact, not in prison.

Stakes get worse as Apollo realizes that accusing Amara would reveal something terrible. Dhurke Sahdmadhi had actually been dead for several days. He had been shot by Minister Inga during the initial rescue of Maya Fey. Amara had channeled him, and that was why she was nowhere to be found at the crime scene. His escape was to release Amara, not from prison but from channeling him.

Maya, on the witness stand, tells everyone that before passing away, Dhurke had asked her to channel his spirit so he could see his son, Apollo, again. Maya kept that promise.

In the final confrontation, Queen Ga’ran takes the prosecutor’s bench herself, using her authority to rewrite the laws of the land in real-time to ensure Apollo’s execution. However, Apollo refuses to give up. With his mentor by his side, they eventually proved that Ga'ran was the true mastermind who murdered Inga because he was planning a coup. They further expose her as the one behind the palace fire twenty-three years ago, an event that claimed the life of Apollo’s biological father, Jove Justice.

The true victory comes in when Apollo proves that Ga’ran possesses no spiritual power at all. Since the Khura’inist throne is strictly reserved for those who can channel spirits, her entire reign is revealed to be a fraud. This revelation instantly strips her of her royal authority, removing the "Defense Culpability Act" (A law passed so that defense attorneys who fail to defend their client would suffer the same punishment as the person they are defending) and causing her own royal guards to turn against her as she suffers a mental breakdown on the stand.

With Ga’ran’s regime gone, the Dark Age of the Law in Khura’in comes to an end, and the legal system begins a slow process of healing. Nahyuta Sahdmadhi, Dhurke’s biological son, is finally liberated from the Queen’s power, and Princess Rayfa begins her journey toward becoming a just and fair ruler. In the end, Apollo decides to stay in Khura’in and honor his foster father’s legacy. He will help rebuild the justice system from the ground up.

Apollo says his goodbyes to his friends and mentor and then goes on to create his own “Justice Law Offices.”

I actually really enjoyed the new setting we are introduced to, Khura'in is such a beautiful place if you ignore the law system. Although I had a hard time with the new mechanics, and yes, dealing with Divination Séances was a pain, I enjoyed the story a lot. It was a very long episode and definitely worth it. Queen Ga'ran was great, although not the best antagonist in the trilogy; she was still a great one. Dhurke's reveal was crazy, and I think I would've reacted more if I didn't somehow jokingly guess it when I was playing.

Thinking about it, the big reveal with spirit channeling is a little bit similar to that of Trials and Tribulations' final case. Maybe that's why I like it. The story was bittersweet in the end. Apollo reconnected with his past, saved his brother from the Queen's manipulations, fulfilled Dhurke's legacy of revolution, said goodbye to his friends, and he even got to see a tiny bit of his biological father again. I think it is a fine ending to Apollo's story, and I really, really want the Athena Cykes trilogy.

My favorite characters were Dhurke and Rayfa. Dhurke is a kind-hearted man and one of the few good fathers in Ace Attorney. Everything he did throughout the case was all for his children. He wanted to see Apollo again and even gave him a picture of his biological father; he wanted to save Nahyuta from the Queen's clutches and make sure he could genuinely smile again. He loved them and Amara so much.

Rayfa, another one of Dhurke's kids, although we don't actually know if Dhurke knew that she was his, I know well that he would love her too. I enjoyed her development from a spoiled princess to someone who genuinely wanted to change and become a better person. She shows guilt when she realizes that her seances were used to falsely accuse rather than bring justice, and she promises she will use her skills to help bring about a better age of law. I know when she grows up, she will be a good queen.

Afterword

Thank you for reading my extremely long yap session about one of my favorite games. This isn't even my favorite game franchise, so yeah. I just talked about cases here, if I talked about my favorite characters with more extent, it might become even longer.

Honorary mention to Franziska von Karma and Bobby Fulbright here, they were amazing characters and my favorite of all games. They were just added to very lacking stories, so I could not mention an episode with them involved that I truly loved, but oh boy, do I enjoy them so much as characters. Do not let the fact that the games they are in aren't as good as the rest fool you. They are still great characters.

Anyway, go play Ace Attorney, or watch gameplays, or do whatever.
