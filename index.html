The Ace Attorney Game Series

Introduction

Ace Attorney is a visual novel adventure game series created by Capcom. It follows the protagonist, defense attorney Phoenix Wright, and his court adventures, where he battles to save his clients. It includes 6 mainline games: the Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney Trilogy and the Apollo Justice: Ace Attorney Trilogy. Plus 4 spin-off games: Ace Attorney Investigations Collection, where our protagonist is prosecutor Miles Edgeworth, and The Great Ace Attorney Chronicles, where our protagonist is Phoenix Wright’s ancestor, Ryunosuke Naruhodo.

I have either played or watched gameplays of all the games except for The Great Ace Attorney Chronicles, but that doesn't mean I can't list down my favorites. I will talk about some of my favorite games in the series, the cases in it which I liked the most, and my favorite characters from them. (The listed games are not in any specific order, I just like them)

There will probably be spoilers.

Phoenix Wright: Trials and Tribulations

"Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney – Trials and Tribulations is a visual novel adventure video game developed and published by Capcom. It was originally released for the Game Boy Advance in 2004 in Japan, and has since been released for several platforms, including a Nintendo DS version that was released in 2007 in Japan and North America, and in 2008 in Europe.

The story follows defense attorneys Phoenix Wright and Mia Fey, who defend their clients in five episodes. Among other characters are Phoenix's assistant and Mia's sister Maya, her cousin Pearl, and prosecutor Godot."

I liked this game a lot, gameplay-wise? It's pretty much the same as the classic Phoenix Wright games, story-wise? It's honestly the best in the trilogy. The episodes of the game were not in chronological order, so you had to put them together yourself. So here are my favorite cases:

Episode 1: Turnabout Memories

The first one, here we play 4 years before the actual setting of Trials and Tribulations. Our main character in the case is none other than Mia Fey, and she is here to defend her client, Phoenix Wright! He is being accused of murdering one of his fellow university students. Our prosecutor in the case is the annoying Winston Payne. Mia defends Phoenix even while the evidence feels so damning. She manages to turn the tide, and the Judge calls for a recess. In the defendant's lobby, Phoenix talks about the necklace he is wearing and how his girlfriend gave it to him as a symbol of their love.

In the second session, the prosecution introduces his witness, the lovely Dahlia Hawthorne. She is Phoenix's girlfriend, and Mia seems to recognize her. Dahlia's statement accuses Phoenix of the murder, and Mia has to hold her case against the accusation, especially tough when everybody in the room seemed to want to protect Dahlia Hawthorne. Mia accuses the witness of lying, and the battle ensues. Phoenix, however, seems to be overprotective of his love, going as far as to interrupt the trial to declare that his "Dollie" would never do anything bad. Sometime later, it is revealed that Dahlia was suspected of the poisoning of the defense attorney, Diego Armando. She was set free after no evidence of poison could be found on her.

Mia soon manages to link the start of Dahlia's relationship with Phoenix to the poisoning, with both having happened 8 months ago. Mia later explains that the necklace Phoenix had held so dear was a bottle of poison, given to him to get rid of evidence. Dahlia had started a relationship with him just to get it back, which soon escalated to her wanting to murder him. The victim of the case used to be friends with Dahlia and worked in a lab, he was the reason Dahlia managed to get her hands on the poison. The victim had started to suspect her of theft of the poison and had gone to warn Phoenix, which ended with his murder. Dahlia soon goes enraged by the accusations, and as the trial progresses, she loses her temper. The case ends with Dahlia being taken into custody and vowing that she and Mia would one day meet again. Phoenix Wright is declared Not Guilty.

I like this case because we get to know a lot more about Mia Fey, who has been dead since the first game. It is also the introduction to a young Phoenix Wright, and it is just lovely to see how he and Mia met each other. The episode is by far the most interesting "tutorial" case of the trilogy, and Dahlia Hawthorne is just a wonderful antagonist.

Episode 5: Bridge to the Turnabout

The last case and the best one yet. Phoenix Wright is invited by Maya Few and Pearl Fey to join them in Hazakura Temple. At first, he doesn't want to, but when he finds that one of the shrine maidens in a magazine featuring the temple looked like Dahlia Hawthorne, his curiosity gets the better of him, and he agrees. They arrive at the temple, and they're welcomed by the head nun, Sister Bikini. Somewhere along the hallway, they meet Elise Deauxnim, a renowned children's book author, who Pearl absolutely adores. When exploring, Phoenix sees his old friend Larry Butz, who is now the apprentice of Ms. Deauxnim. Later, Maya and Phoenix trekked the old Dusky Bridge to check out the Inner Temple, the place Maya was supposed to stay in for her spirit medium training. There they meet Iris, a temple nun, and the same woman Phoenix saw in the magazine, both Phoenix and Iris are shocked to see each other. Dinner soon rolls by, and then bedtime. Phoenix tries to sleep and finds out he needs to use the bathroom. While outside, he meets Ms. Deauxnim, who tells him she is looking for Pearl. Phoenix then stumbles upon Iris and tries to ask her questions, she excuses herself and gives Phoenix her hood as a gift. She says goodbye and uses Phoenix's name, something that surprises him because he never told her that. Five Psyche-locks appear to guard the secret. (Phoenix has this thing called a magatama where he can basically see when people are keeping secrets. Psyche-Locks appear to guard the secret, and the more there are, the deeper it is.)
